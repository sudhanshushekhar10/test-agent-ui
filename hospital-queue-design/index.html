Smart Queue Ticket Interface for Hospitals

A user-friendly interface for hospital queue management

Design Overview

This interface allows patients to book OPD appointments online, view their position in real-time, and receive notifications when their turn is approaching. The design follows accessibility guidelines and maintains a clean, minimal approach suitable for users of all ages.

Design Justification

  • Accessibility-First Design (WCAG 2.1): High contrast ratios, large touch targets (44Ã—44px minimum), clear visual hierarchy, and consistent navigation ensure the app is usable for elderly patients and those with disabilities.
  • Progressive Disclosure (Nielsen Norman Group): Breaking the booking process into logical steps reduces cognitive load and decision fatigue, particularly important in healthcare contexts where users may be stressed.
  • Status Visibility & Feedback (Jakob Nielsen): Real-time queue position updates, progress indicators, and clear notifications keep users informed and reduce anxiety during waiting periods.
  • Minimalist Design (Material Design & Apple HIG): Limited color palette, strategic use of white space, and focus on essential content create a calm, uncluttered experience appropriate for a healthcare setting.
  • Consistent Navigation & Mental Models: Familiar UI patterns and components that match platform conventions reduce the learning curve for less tech-savvy users, leveraging existing mental models.
